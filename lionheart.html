TROY HANCOCK

Gameplay Engineer
- - -

Lionheart Games (later rebranded to Starmi) was an indie mobile game studio in Atlanta, Georgia, founded by ex-Hi-Rez Studios developers.

I joined early in the studio’s lifespan and spent about two years there, contributing to a small but highly adaptable team that ranged from 7 to 25 people. As with many startup environments, I wore multiple hats—though my primary focus was Gameplay Engineering. I was responsible for designing and implementing 50+ of the core abilities in Dragonspire using Unreal Engine’s Gameplay Ability System (GAS), as well as creating AI-driven boss fights. Additionally, I worked on various backend systems and contributed across disciplines, including game design, UI, tech art, and build maintenance.

Dragonspire was a mobile-first, top-down RPG with anime-inspired visuals. While the studio shut down before the game’s mobile release, an early access version was launched on PC in its final weeks. (See gameplay trailer below.)

Relevant Technologies: C++, Visual Studio, Unreal Engine 4, G.A.S. (Gameplay Ability System, UE plugin), Android Studio, Perforce, Jira, Notion, Jenkins Build Manager
